Our Story

We met our first year at Whitman College, where we were placed in the same required intro liberal arts class. We were the only two who actually participated in discussions, with Walter often debating Skylar’s answers. As the year progressed, we starting spending more and more time together outside of class. Skylar eventually worked up the nerve to ask for Walter’s number, who, in a Rolling Rock-induced haze, typed in the only phone number he was sure he remembered - his parents’ landline. All was quickly worked out, but Skylar still loves to hold this over Walter.

Things really kicked off when Walter had Skylar over to his dorm room for an evening, only to sit as far away from her as he could. Walter maintains this was a show of respect, Skylar maintains he was just a chicken. We talked all night and realized we had something more than friendship.

After graduating college, we moved down to Tempe, Arizona, where Skylar got her Ph.D. in Astrophysics and Walter faffed about (and got his Masters). Skylar convinced Walter to get a cat, and although Atlas is an idiot we do love him very much.

After seven years together, Walter realized it might be time to take the next step. He proposed to Skylar on a surprise trip to Redwood National Park, the site of our first road trip together back in 2019. Now that Skylar’s wrapped up her PhD, we’re preparing to move to Ontario for her new job and our next adventure.

We’ve been lucky to have so many years together as partners and friends, and now we’re excited to celebrate with you all as we begin the next chapter!

Schedule

Our wedding will take place on the afternoon and evening of Saturday, May 22, 2027, at Aspen Hall in Bend Oregon. We hope you will join us for other festivities around the big day! We will be planning out activities for Friday and Sunday, with more details coming soon.

Travel and Lodging

Flying In

The Bend/Redmond airport (RDM) is about a 20 minute drive from downtown Bend, and a 30 minute drive from Aspen Hall.

Where to Stay

There are plenty of lodging options in Bend including hotels and airbnbs. More detailed recommendations will be coming soon.

Getting Around

If you choose to rent a car, Bend is very easy to explore, just be prepared for a lot of roundabouts! We are working on coordinating transportation to/from the wedding venue; stay tuned for more details.

Things to do

Bend is a lovely town with plenty to explore both in the city and in the surrounding wilderness. We will be planning a few activities over the course of the weekend, but you can also take time to go hiking, tour local breweries, or float the river!

FAQs

Here you'll find your answers to all questions wedding related!

Logistics

Why is your wedding website so cool and unique?

The short answer: Skylar was procrastinating working on her PhD dissertation. The long answer: Skylar thought it would be fun to learn more html and so decided to build the wedding website herself. Will this completely implode on us down the line and turn out to be a very bad idea? Maybe! But we're trying it anyway.

What is my deadline to RSVP?

We've got plenty of time to the wedding...once invites are sent out our link to RSVP will go live and you will have until early spring 2027.

Can I bring a date?

Our venue is small and limits the number of guests. Please only bring a date if your invitation explicitly grants you a plus one!

Can I bring my kids?

Children listed on the invitation are welcome at both the ceremony and reception.

How can I get to the venue?

We are currently working on setting up coordinated transportation for guests from the hotel. Aspen Hall is only ten minutes from downtown Bend, and easy to get to with an uber or taxi. Our ceremony and reception are happening at the same place, so once you're there, you don't need to worry about moving around! Check out the schedule page for more details.

What's the dress code?

Walter specifically requests Bend-core: flannels, patagonia vests, and berkenstocks only. Skylar specifically requests that you ignore him and wear something at least kind of nice. Just not white. Note for the ladies: ceremony will be on a grass lawn and dancing on a patio so we don't reccomend skinny heels.

Is the wedding indoors or outdoors?

Our ceremony will be outdoors, and the reception will take place both inside and out, weather permitting. Weather in Bend in May is in general temperate, although it could get down into the 50s in the evening so bring layers.

What's on the menu?

It's a secret (AKA we haven't picked a caterer yet).

Will there be an open bar?

Yes!

Can I take pictures during the ceremony?

We will have a professional photographer capturing our day, so please don't take photos during the ceremony itself to avoid disruptions. However, you are welcome to capture moments over the rest of the evening!

Getting ahead of awkward conversations:

Is anyone changing their name?

Nope!

Are you doing wedding parties?

Nope! We've been together for many years and our friend group is completely integrated so we didn't want to make people pick sides. And we want all our friends to be able to completely enjoy the wedding and be in the moment!

Can I give a toast?

So that we can have as much time to party and hang out with you as possible, the only toasts for the evening will be by our parents. However, you are welcome to come up and say nice things to us at any point in the day :)

Can we give cash gifts?

Yes!!! (but not in a desparate way)

How late are you going to make us stay up?

Due to venue limits and the fact that we are not night owls we are planning on wrapping up our celebrations around 10pm. We would love if you could stay and enjoy the dancing and conversation, but also considering we have never stayed the whole time at the weddings we've been to we certainly don't expect it from you.
